Etymology

The name ferret is derived from the Latin word furittus, which translates to little thief, a reference to ferrets' common habbit of sneaking off with various small items.

Ferrets

Ferrets are mammals who belong to the genus Mustela of the Mustelidae family. Ferrets have been domesticated for at least 2500 years. They have been used to hunt rabbits, and in some parts of the world they still are. Nowadays, however, they are mostly kept as pets. Ferrets average 20 inches in length and weigh anywhere between 1.5lbs to 4lbs. They have a natural lifespan of 7 to 10 years.

Fun Facts

A group of ferrets is called a “business”.

Ferrets sleep between 14 to 18 hours a day.

Ferrets are crepuscular, which means they are most active at dusk and dawn.
